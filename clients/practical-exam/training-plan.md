## Progression Guidelines

- **Weeks 1-4 (Accumulation):** When all prescribed reps are completed at RPE 8 or below for two consecutive sessions, increase load by the smallest available increment (typically 2.5 kg for upper body, 2.5-5 kg for lower body).
- **Week 5 (Deload):** Drop 1-2 sets per exercise, keep weight the same. Focus on movement quality and recovery.
- **Weeks 6-8 (Intensification):** Resume full volume. Continue progressive overload. Push RPE to 8-9 on main compounds.
- If a rep range is not met at the current weight for two sessions, reduce load by 5-10% and rebuild.

## Notes

- **Scoliosis:** Unilateral exercises (Bulgarian split squats, single-arm rows, lunges) are included to help address potential side-to-side imbalances. If any exercise causes discomfort on one side, reduce load or range of motion for that side and note it in the log.
- **Anterior Pelvic Tilt:** Deadbugs are programmed twice per week for core stability and pelvic control. Hip flexor stretches are included in every lower body cooldown. Focus on posterior pelvic tilt cues during hip thrusts and planks.
- **Boxing:** The once-weekly boxing session provides additional conditioning and upper body work. Schedule it on Wednesday or a non-training day for best recovery.
- **Rest Days:** Wednesday and weekends. Adjust day assignments as needed while maintaining at least one rest day between lower body sessions.
