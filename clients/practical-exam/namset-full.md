
# Couch Stretch

**N – Name:** Couch Stretch

**A – Area:** Lower body (hip flexors / quads)

**M – Muscles Involved:**  
Primary: Hip flexors, Quadriceps  
Secondary: Glutes, Core stabilizers

**S – Silent Demonstration:**  
Kneel with one knee against a wall or couch and the other foot planted in front. Torso upright. Gently shift hips forward while keeping chest tall.

**E – Explanation / Key Technical Points:**  
Maintain upright posture. Engage glutes slightly to open the hip. Avoid arching the lower back.

**T – Teach the Exercise:**  
Verbal cues:

- “Tall chest.”
    
- “Squeeze the glute of the kneeling leg.”
    
- “Slowly push hips forward.”
    

Visual cue: Demonstrate upright torso.  
Tactile cue: Light tap on glute to encourage activation.

# World's Greatest Stretch

**N – Name:** World's Greatest Stretch

**A – Area:** Full body mobility

**M – Muscles Involved:**  
Primary: Hip flexors, Hamstrings  
Secondary: Thoracic spine rotators, Glutes

**S – Silent Demonstration:**  
Step into a deep lunge. Both hands to the floor. Rotate torso upward reaching one arm to the ceiling.

**E – Explanation / Key Technical Points:**  
Keep front knee over ankle. Rotate through upper back rather than lower back.

**T – Teach the Exercise:**  
Verbal cues:

- “Big step forward.”
    
- “Chest rotates to the ceiling.”
    
- “Keep hips low.”
    

Visual cue: Show controlled rotation.  
Tactile cue: Guide shoulder into rotation.

# Glute Bridges

**N – Name:** Glute Bridge

**A – Area:** Posterior chain (lower body)

**M – Muscles Involved:**  
Primary: Glutes, Hamstrings  
Secondary: Core, Lower back

**S – Silent Demonstration:**  
Lie on back with knees bent. Drive hips upward until body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees.

**E – Explanation / Key Technical Points:**  
Push through heels. Keep ribs down. Pause briefly at the top.

**T – Teach the Exercise:**  
Verbal cues:

- “Drive through your heels.”
    
- “Squeeze glutes at the top.”
    
- “Lower slowly.”
    

Visual cue: Demonstrate full hip extension.  
Tactile cue: Tap glutes to encourage activation.
