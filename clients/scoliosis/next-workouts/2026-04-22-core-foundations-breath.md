# Next Workout: Core Foundations & Breath

First session of the 12-week plan. Keep everything at the lowest end of the prescribed range — the goal today is to feel the movements, not to challenge them. Stop any exercise that produces back pain or signals a spasm; mark it in the log.

## Warm-Up (3 min)

- Supine diaphragmatic breathing — knees bent, feet flat. 8 slow breaths. On each inhale, imagine expanding into the **right side** of your ribcage (the concave side) and pressing it gently into the floor.
- Pelvic tilts, supine x 8. Small range — tilt pelvis toward ribs, then away. No pain.

## Session

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Weight | Rest | Notes |
|----------|------|------|--------|------|-------|
| Dead bug (slow, breath-led) | 3 | 6 each side | Bodyweight | 45s | Est. starting. Exhale as the limb extends. Low back stays in contact with the floor — if it lifts, reduce limb range. |
| Bird dog | 3 | 6 each side | Bodyweight | 45s | Est. starting. Pause 2s at full extension. Pelvis stays level — don't let the hip drop or lift. |
| Side plank from knees | 2 | 20s hold each side | Bodyweight | 45s | Est. starting. The left (convex) side will likely feel weaker — normal. Don't sag at the hip. |
| Supine 90/90 heel taps | 2 | 8 each side | Bodyweight | 45s | Est. starting. Tap heel lightly to floor, return. Keep low back flat. |

## Progression Notes

- All exercises are bodyweight — no weight decisions this session.
- These are all Est. starting loads/volumes. Log how each felt (RPE 1–10) so next week's Day 1 session can progress appropriately.
- If any movement triggers spasm or sciatica, stop it and note it in the log — it will be dropped or regressed next session.
- Dead bug and bird dog in particular can aggravate the lower back if the pelvis isn't controlled. If either feels like it's loading the lumbar spine, shrink the range of motion.

## Cooldown (3 min)

- Single-leg knee-to-chest x 30s each side — gentle decompression for the lumbar spine. Spend the longer hold on the left (sciatica) side.
- Child's pose, reaching both arms to the right x 45s — opens the right/concave side of the ribcage and mirrors the physio's elongation work.
