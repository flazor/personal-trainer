# Training Plan: Daily 20-Minute Home Program for Congenital Thoracic Scoliosis

## Overview

This plan is a daily 20-minute home companion to the client's existing scoliosis physiotherapy (Schroth-style breathing and spinal elongation) and 1:1 reformer Pilates. It does **not** replace that specialised care — it extends it into daily practice with low-load, low-risk movements.

Design principles:
- **Complement, don't duplicate physio.** The physio prescribes the asymmetric/curve-specific work. This plan provides general core, mobility, glute, and balance work that supports what the physio is already doing.
- **Low spinal compression.** No heavy axial loading (no loaded squats, no conventional deadlifts, no overhead pressing with load). This protects fused vertebrae and the existing curve.
- **Slow on-ramp.** Muscle spasms are triggered by novelty — new exercises start at low reps and the lowest end of the range, progress only when the previous session was comfortable 24 hours later.
- **Weight-bearing for hip bone density.** Mild osteopenia in the hip benefits from standing/loaded work (carries, step-ups, single-leg balance) more than machine-based isolation.
- **Breath is core.** Diaphragmatic breathing (expanding into the right/concave side) is the thread that runs through every session.
- **Pair with cardio, don't replace it.** The 10–15 km trail running, 20–30 km cycling, and 1.5 km swimming continue as-is — they are excellent for this profile.

A week rotates through six distinct focuses plus one restorative day. Each day is ~20 min: 3-min warm-up, 12–14 min work, 3–5 min cooldown.

## Weekly Schedule

### Day 1 — Monday: Core Foundations & Breath

**Warm-up** (3 min)
- Diaphragmatic breathing, supine, knees bent — 8 slow breaths expanding the right-side ribcage into the floor
- Pelvic tilts x 8

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest | Notes |
|----------|------|------|------|-------|
| Dead bug (slow, breath-led) | 3 | 6 each side | 45s | Ribs heavy, low back to floor, exhale on limb extension |
| Bird dog | 3 | 6 each side | 45s | Pause 2s at full extension, keep pelvis level |
| Side plank from knees | 2 | 20s hold each side | 45s | Convex (left) side may feel weaker — that's expected |
| Supine 90/90 heel taps | 2 | 8 each side | 45s | Small range, no low-back arch |

**Cooldown** (3 min)
- Knee-to-chest single-leg x 30s each side
- Child's pose with right-side reach x 45s (opens concave side)

### Day 2 — Tuesday: Hip Mobility & Lower Back Relief

**Warm-up** (3 min)
- Cat-cow x 8
- Standing hip circles x 5 each direction per leg

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest | Notes |
|----------|------|------|------|-------|
| 90/90 hip switches | 2 | 6 each side | 30s | Slow and controlled |
| Supine figure-4 stretch | 1 | 45s each side | 15s | Hold longer on left (sciatica side) |
| Couch stretch (half-kneeling hip flexor) | 1 | 45s each side | 15s | Squeeze glute on kneeling side |
| Thread the needle | 2 | 6 each side | 30s | Thoracic rotation, gentle |
| McKenzie press-up (if comfortable) | 2 | 6 | 30s | Skip if lower back pain flares |

**Cooldown** (3 min)
- Legs-up-wall x 2 min with slow breathing

### Day 3 — Wednesday: Posterior Chain Strength

**Warm-up** (3 min)
- Glute bridge x 10, tempo: 2s up, 2s hold, 2s down
- Banded lateral walks x 10 steps each direction

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest | Notes |
|----------|------|------|------|-------|
| Glute bridge, feet elevated on couch/step | 3 | 10 | 45s | Pause 2s at top, squeeze both glutes evenly |
| Banded clamshell | 3 | 12 each side | 30s | Hips stacked, no pelvic rotation |
| Bird dog with pause | 3 | 8 each side | 45s | 3s hold at full extension |
| Wall sit | 2 | 30–45s | 45s | Neutral spine, avoid thoracic flexion |

**Cooldown** (3 min)
- Pigeon pose or supine figure-4 x 45s each side
- Standing forward fold, knees soft x 45s

### Day 4 — Thursday: Thoracic Mobility & Flexibility

**Warm-up** (3 min)
- Cat-cow x 8
- Open books (side-lying thoracic rotation) x 6 each side

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest | Notes |
|----------|------|------|------|-------|
| Foam roller thoracic extension (or rolled towel) | 2 | 6 slow breaths per spot, 3 spots | 30s | Avoid rolling over fused segments — stick to mid/lower thoracic |
| Quadruped thoracic rotation | 2 | 6 each side | 30s | Reach hand to ceiling, exhale into rotation |
| Doorway chest opener | 2 | 30s each arm | 15s | Keep ribs down |
| Seated side bend with reach (toward right side) | 2 | 5 each side | 30s | Longer hold on the right (concave) side to lengthen |
| Puppy pose | 1 | 45s | — | Arms long, chest toward floor |

**Cooldown** (3 min)
- Supine spinal twist x 45s each side
- Diaphragmatic breathing x 2 min

### Day 5 — Friday: Asymmetric Core & Anti-Rotation

**Warm-up** (3 min)
- Dead bug x 6 each side
- Standing band pull-apart x 10

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest | Notes |
|----------|------|------|------|-------|
| Pallof press (band, half-kneeling) | 3 | 8 each side | 45s | Resist rotation, breathe out on press |
| Side plank (full or from knees) | 3 | 20–30s each side | 45s | Prioritise quality over hold duration |
| Suitcase hold (single dumbbell or kettlebell) | 2 | 20s each side | 45s | Ribs stacked over pelvis, don't lean |
| Bird dog slow tempo | 2 | 6 each side | 30s | 5s extension, 5s return |

**Cooldown** (3 min)
- Child's pose with arms reaching right x 45s
- Supine breathing, hands on right lower ribs, breathe into the right hand x 2 min

### Day 6 — Saturday: Standing Balance & Bone Loading

**Warm-up** (3 min)
- Ankle circles x 10 each foot
- Bodyweight mini-squats x 10
- March in place x 30s

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest | Notes |
|----------|------|------|------|-------|
| Step-ups (stair or low box) | 3 | 8 each leg | 45s | Drive through mid-foot, control descent |
| Single-leg balance (eyes open → eyes closed) | 3 | 30s each leg | 30s | Progress to eyes closed when stable 30s eyes open |
| Farmer's carry (two dumbbells or grocery bags) | 3 | 20m or 30s | 60s | Tall posture, steady breath |
| Calf raises | 2 | 15 | 30s | Double-leg; progress to single-leg when ready |
| Goblet squat to chair (light weight) | 2 | 10 | 60s | Sit to touch, stand — no deep squat |

**Cooldown** (3 min)
- Standing quad stretch x 30s each side
- Downward dog to child's pose x 3 cycles

### Day 7 — Sunday: Restorative Breath & Release

**Warm-up** (2 min)
- Diaphragmatic breathing seated x 10 breaths

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest | Notes |
|----------|------|------|------|-------|
| Side-lying breathing into right ribs | 1 | 10 breaths each side | — | Longer hold on the left-down position (right ribs up) to open concave side |
| Foam roll upper back (mid-thoracic only) | 1 | 60s | — | Avoid fused segments and lumbar |
| Supported fish pose (pillow or rolled towel across mid-back) | 1 | 2 min | — | Relax, breathe wide |
| Legs-up-the-wall | 1 | 3 min | — | Parasympathetic reset |
| Supine spinal twist | 1 | 60s each side | — | Gentle, no forcing |
| Happy baby or knees to chest | 1 | 60s | — | Decompress lumbar |

**Cooldown** — integrated into the session above.

## Progression Guidelines

**Weeks 1–4 (Foundation).** Stay at the rep/time minimums listed. The goal is to establish the daily habit and learn each movement. No progression in weeks 1–4 — if a session feels too easy, keep it easy.

**Weeks 5–8 (Build).** Progress one of these per exercise when last session felt like RPE 6 or lower:
- Core holds: add 10s
- Strength moves: add 2 reps per set OR add a set
- Carries/step-ups: add a small amount of weight (0.5–1 kg) or extra 10s/distance
- Balance: progress eyes-open → eyes-closed, or add a head turn

**Weeks 9–12 (Consolidate).** Continue progression. Add a second set to any 2-set exercise that feels too easy. Introduce harder regressions (e.g. full side plank if from-knees is solid; single-leg calf raise).

**Deload.** Every 4 weeks (end of weeks 4, 8, 12) run Day 7 content for 3 consecutive days — lighter mobility/breath, no strength work. Resume the plan after.

**Spasm protocol.** If a back spasm triggers during or within 24h of a session:
1. Skip strength work for 48h; substitute Day 7 restorative content.
2. When resuming, drop the triggering exercise by one regression (e.g. side plank from knees → side plank with top foot on floor) or reduce reps by 30%.
3. If the same exercise triggers a spasm twice, remove it from the plan and flag it for the physio to review.

## Notes

- **Consult the physio.** Before starting, share this plan with the scoliosis physio. Ask specifically whether any of these movements conflict with the Schroth protocol or should be modified for the fused thoracic segments.
- **Equipment assumptions.** Yoga mat, long resistance band, loop resistance band (light/medium), one pair of light dumbbells (2–5 kg) or equivalent household weights, a pillow or rolled towel, a chair, and optionally a foam roller. Nothing else is required.
- **Cardio stays as-is.** Continue trail running (10–15 km/wk), cycling (20–30 km/wk), swimming (1.5 km/wk), and daily walking. These are protective for both the spine and hip bone density. No change recommended.
- **Reformer Pilates is the asymmetric primary.** The 1:1 reformer sessions do the targeted curve-specific strengthening. This home plan intentionally keeps general — don't try to replicate the asymmetric Pilates work at home without supervision.
- **HRT and bone health.** HRT is helpful for bone density alongside this program's weight-bearing work. No training modification needed.
- **Session timing flexibility.** If a day gets missed, don't double up — just resume on the current day of the week. The rotation is designed so any single missed day is not catastrophic.
- **Run/ride days.** A 20-min home session on the same day as a run or ride is fine; do the home session either before (as movement prep) or several hours after.
