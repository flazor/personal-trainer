# Training Plan: Strength Foundations for Tim

## Overview

This is an 8-week full-body strength program built around two training days per week (Tuesday and Friday). As a gym beginner at age 50 with a strong aerobic base from running, cycling, climbing, and surfing, you'll benefit from linear progression on the core compound lifts.

**Approach**: Linear periodization — start with moderate rep ranges to build movement proficiency, then shift toward heavier loading as technique solidifies. Each session is full-body to maximize training frequency per muscle group on only 2 days per week.

**Anterior pelvic tilt accommodation**: Every session includes hip flexor stretching (couch stretch) and glute activation (glute bridges) as part of the warm-up. Back barbell squat is used instead of front squat per your movement assessment recommendation. Extra glute and core work is programmed throughout.

**Weeks 1-4**: Build movement quality at RPE 7 (3 reps in reserve). Learn the lifts.
**Weeks 5-8**: Increase intensity to RPE 8 (2 reps in reserve). Push toward strength gains.
**Deload**: Week 9 or as needed — reduce volume by 50%, keep movements the same.

## Weekly Schedule

### Day 1 — Tuesday: Full Body (Bench Focus)

**Warm-up** (10 min)
- 5 min light rowing or cycling
- Couch stretch: 60s per side
- Glute bridges: 2 x 10 (bodyweight, squeeze at top)
- Band pull-aparts: 2 x 15
- Arm circles and thoracic rotations: 1 min each
- 2 light warm-up sets of bench press (20 kg bar, then 35 kg)

| Exercise                   | Sets | Reps    | Rest  | Notes                                                                        |
| -------------------------- | ---- | ------- | ----- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Barbell Bench Press        | 4    | 5       | 3 min | Start at 50 kg (71% 1RM). Add 2.5 kg/week. RPE 7                             |
| Back Barbell Squat         | 3    | 8       | 2 min | Start at 30 kg. Controlled tempo, full depth if comfortable. Add 2.5 kg/week |
| Dumbbell Row (one-arm)     | 3    | 10/side | 90s   | Start at 12.5 kg. Pull to hip, squeeze shoulder blade at top                 |
| Trap Bar Deadlift          | 3    | 8       | 2 min | Start at 50 kg. Drive through floor, neutral spine, lockout with glutes      |
| Hanging Knee Raises        | 3    | 10-12   | 60s   | Captain's chair or pull-up bar. Controlled raise and lower — no swinging. Builds hip flexor awareness and anti-extension core for APT |
| Hip Thrust                 | 3    | 12      | 60s   | Start at 20 kg (bar only). Key glute strengthener for APT correction         |

**Cooldown** (5 min)
- Couch stretch: 60s per side
- Pigeon stretch: 60s per side
- Chest doorway stretch: 30s per side
- Foam roll quads and hip flexors: 2 min

### Day 2 — Friday: Full Body (Squat & Pull Focus)

**Warm-up** (10 min)
- 5 min light rowing or cycling
- Couch stretch: 60s per side
- Glute bridges: 2 x 10 (bodyweight, squeeze at top)
- Leg swings (front/back and lateral): 10 each direction per leg
- Bodyweight squats: 2 x 10
- 2 light warm-up sets of squat (20 kg bar, then 25 kg)

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest | Notes |
|----------|------|------|------|-------|
| Back Barbell Squat | 4 | 5 | 3 min | Start at 30 kg. Add 2.5 kg/week. RPE 7 |
| Incline Dumbbell Bench Press | 3 | 8 | 90s | Start at 14 kg per hand. Supports bench 1RM from a different angle |
| Lat Pulldown | 3 | 8 | 90s | Start at 30 kg. Wide grip — builds pulling strength for surfing pop-up |
| Dumbbell Lunges | 3 | 10/leg | 90s | Start at 8 kg per hand. Single-leg stability, glute activation |
| Face Pulls | 3 | 15 | 60s | Start at 20 kg on cable. Shoulder health and upper back posture |

**Cooldown** (5 min)
- Couch stretch: 60s per side
- Hamstring stretch (standing or lying): 60s per side
- Cat-cow: 10 slow reps
- Foam roll glutes and thoracic spine: 2 min

## Progression Guidelines

**Weeks 1-4 (Learning Phase)**
- Start all lifts at the weights listed above — prioritize form over load
- Bench press: start at 50 kg, add 2.5 kg per week (targeting 57.5 kg by week 4)
- Squat: start at 30 kg, add 2.5 kg per week (targeting 37.5 kg by week 4)
- Accessory lifts: increase weight when you can complete all prescribed reps at RPE 7 or below
- Target RPE 7 (3 reps in reserve) on all working sets

**Weeks 5-8 (Strength Phase)**
- Bench press: continue adding 2.5 kg/week (targeting 60-67.5 kg for sets of 5)
- Squat: continue adding 2.5 kg/week (targeting 40-47.5 kg for sets of 5)
- Increase to RPE 8 (2 reps in reserve) on primary lifts
- Accessory work stays at RPE 7-8

**Deload (Week 9)**
- Perform all the same exercises
- Reduce working weights by 40-50%
- Reduce sets to 2 per exercise
- Focus on movement quality and recovery

**When to add weight**: If you complete all prescribed sets and reps and feel you had 2+ reps left in the tank, add 2.5 kg next session for that exercise. For dumbbells, go up to the next available pair (usually 2 kg jumps).

## Notes

**Starting Weight Philosophy**
- All weights above are deliberately conservative — you should feel like you could do more
- The first 2 weeks are about learning movement patterns, not testing limits
- If any weight feels too heavy to maintain good form, drop it down without hesitation
- If any weight feels far too easy (RPE 5 or below), add 2.5-5 kg next session

**Anterior Pelvic Tilt Management**
- Couch stretches and glute bridges are programmed into every warm-up — don't skip these
- Hip thrusts, dead bugs, and hanging knee raises directly address the weak glutes and poor pelvic control
- If you feel your lower back taking over during squats or deadlifts, reduce weight and focus on bracing your core

**Surfing Pop-Up Performance**
- The combination of bench press strength, lat pulldown pulling power, and explosive hip extension from hip thrusts and squats will directly transfer to a faster pop-up
- Consider adding 2-3 explosive push-ups at the end of your warm-up on Tuesdays once comfortable (weeks 3+)

**Recovery at 50**
- You have a strong aerobic base which helps recovery, but allow 48+ hours between gym sessions
- Your Tuesday/Friday schedule provides ideal spacing
- Sleep and nutrition are critical — aim for 7-8 hours sleep and adequate protein (1.6-2.0 g/kg = 112-140g daily)
- If joints feel stiff, extend the warm-up by a few minutes

**Bench Press 1RM Goal**
- Current 1RM: 70 kg. With consistent linear progression at 2.5 kg/week, your working weights for sets of 5 should reach ~65-70 kg by week 8, suggesting a new 1RM in the 80-85 kg range
- Test your 1RM after the deload week (week 9) when fully recovered

**Continuing Your Other Sports**
- Keep running, cycling, climbing, and surfing as you enjoy them
- If you notice gym recovery suffering, slightly reduce the volume of endurance activities on the day before gym sessions
